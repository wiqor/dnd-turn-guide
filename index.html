This is a Turn Guide for you during combat. There's a lot to keep track of, so I made this to help you.

Key =
[A] = Action. Its an action you can take, using 1 Action on your turn. Each [A] has a little drop-down arrow next to it. Click it to expand, and see the different options you have for that kind of Action.
[B] = Bonus Action. Its a bonus action you can take, using 1 Bonus Action on your turn. Same rules for Actions, they have little dropdowns to check out and choose.
[R] = Reaction. Its an action you can take, using 1 Reaction, NOT on your turn.
[U] = Resource/Use. Its a resource you can use on your turn, and it doesn't cost anything to use. Its free! You can use as many resources on your turn as you like. Each resource will explain how to use it, when you can use it, and what it does.
Blue is for Gear, like Weapons, Armor, or Items. You'll see them as options for things to choose, and within notes to show that item's effect on your choice of action.
[Roll] = Rolling Dice. If you see Yellow, roll some dice! You'll see which kind of dice to roll, and what to add to it.
Green is for Features, Traits, and Abilities.
Navy is for Passive Effects, things like increases to your speed or bonuses you get to rolls. There's a section just for Passive Effects. Always check it before you roll dice, you might have a bonus you didn't know about!
Purple is for Free Actions, things you can do that don't require anything to use, although you can only take one Free Action on your turn.

In general, there are many things you can do in combat other than attacking.

  • I'm about to roll, can I boost it? (Check Attacks, Bonus Actions, & Passives)
  • I hit, can I make it hit harder? (Check Attacks, Resources, & Passives)
  • I missed, can I fix it? (Check Resources)
  • I and/or an ally got hit, can I react? (Check Reactions)

[A] Attack

  • Extra Attack (Fighter Feature, src): You can attack three times whenever you take the Attack Action on your turn.
  • Unleash Incarnation (Echo Knight Freature, src): You can make 1 additional Attack from your Echo Knight's position whenever you take the Attack Action on your turn.
  • Windswept Wings, Wind's Howl: While flying, you have advantage on weapon attacks made against enemies on the ground.
  • Stormcaller's Echo (DM Bonus Feature): You CRIT on a 19 or 20. When you kill a creature with a CRIT hit, creatures of your choice within 30ft make a WIS Save against your (CHA) Spell Save DC or be Frightened for 1 minute. They can repeat the Save on their turn using an Action.
  • Fighting Style, Blind Fighting (Fighter Feature): You have blindsight, range of 10ft. Within that range, you can effectively see anything that isn't behind total cover, even if you're blinded or in darkness. Moreover, you can see an invisible creature within that range, unless the creature successfully hides from you.

[A] Disengage

  • If you take the Disengage action, your movement doesn’t provoke opportunity attacks for the rest of the turn.

[A] Dodge

  • When you take the Dodge action, you focus entirely on avoiding attacks. Until the start of your next turn, any attack roll made against you has disadvantage if you can see the attacker, and you make Dexterity saving throws with advantage.

[A] Hide

  • When you take the Hide action, you make a Stealth check in an attempt to hide, following the rules for hiding. If you succeed, you gain certain benefits, as described in the “Unseen Attackers and Targets” section.

[A] Help

  • When you take the Help action, the creature you aid gains advantage on the next ability check it makes to perform the task you are helping with, provided that it makes the check before the start of your next turn.
  • Alternatively, you can aid a friendly creature in attacking a creature within 5 feet of you. You feint, distract the target, or in some other way team up to make your ally’s attack more effective. If your ally attacks the target before your next turn, the first attack roll is made with advantage.

[A] Ready

  • The Ready Action allows you to use an Action as a Reaction in response to a trigger.
  • First, define the trigger. (Example, "If that goblin moves close to me,")
  • Then, define the Action you'll take in response. (Example, "then I'll Attack him.")
  • You take this Reaction before the start of your next turn. If the trigger happens, you can choose to ignore it.

[F] Free Action

⤷ [F] Object Interaction

⤷ [F] Ability Check

  • You can make an Ability Check during your turn, except for the following:
  • Investigation Checks require using the Search Action to find something.
  • Stealth Checks require using the Hide Action to conceal yourself.
  • Medicine Checks require an Action to stabilize a friend.
